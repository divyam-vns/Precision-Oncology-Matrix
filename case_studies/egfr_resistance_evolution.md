
# 🧬 Case Study 3: EGFR Resistance Evolution in NSCLC

## Primary Oncogenic Driver
EGFR activating mutations:
- Exon 19 deletions
- L858R point mutation

These mutations drive constitutive kinase activation → uncontrolled proliferation.

---

## First-Line Therapy Response
- 1st/2nd generation TKIs:
  - Erlotinib
  - Gefitinib
  - Afatinib

Initial response is strong due to EGFR dependence.

---

## Resistance Evolution (Key Mechanism)
### T790M Mutation
- “Gatekeeper” mutation in EGFR kinase domain
- Increases ATP affinity → reduces TKI binding
- Causes acquired resistance to 1st/2nd generation TKIs

---

## 3rd Generation Therapy
- Osimertinib (AZD9291)
- Irreversibly binds mutant EGFR (including T790M)
- Effective in T790M-positive tumors

---

## Secondary Resistance (Critical Insight)
### C797S Mutation
- Prevents covalent binding of osimertinib
- Represents resistance to 3rd-generation TKIs

---

## Therapeutic Challenge
C797S creates a major clinical bottleneck:
- Tumor becomes resistant to both early and late-generation TKIs depending on allele configuration (cis/trans)

---

## Bypass Signaling Mechanisms
Tumors may escape EGFR dependency via:
- MET amplification
- HER2 activation
- KRAS pathway reactivation
- Small cell transformation

---

## Emerging 4th-Generation Strategies
- Allosteric EGFR inhibitors
- Combination therapies targeting bypass pathways
- Dual inhibition strategies (EGFR + MET)

---

## Clinical Insight
EGFR resistance is not linear — it is evolutionary:
Target pressure → mutation selection → pathway bypass → lineage plasticity
