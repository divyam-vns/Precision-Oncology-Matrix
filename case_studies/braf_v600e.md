
# 🧬 Case Study 1: BRAF V600E – MAPK Pathway Activation

## Biological Mechanism
BRAF V600E is a constitutively activating mutation in the MAPK pathway.

Normal signaling:
EGFR → RAS → BRAF → MEK → ERK → proliferation

Mutation effect:
V600E bypasses upstream regulation → constant MEK/ERK activation

---

## Clinical Impact
- Strong oncogenic driver in melanoma, colorectal cancer, thyroid cancer
- Predicts sensitivity to BRAF inhibitors

---

## Targeted Therapy
- Dabrafenib (BRAF inhibitor)
- Trametinib (MEK inhibitor)

Combination rationale:
→ prevents MAPK pathway reactivation
→ delays resistance

---

## Resistance Mechanisms
- MEK reactivation
- KRAS amplification
- Alternate MAPK bypass signaling

---

## Clinical Insight
Dual inhibition is required due to rapid pathway reactivation.
